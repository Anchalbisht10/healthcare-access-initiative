# Healthcare Access Initiative

## The Problem

Millions of people in rural India cannot access basic healthcare 
when they need it. Rural areas hold 70% of India's population 
but are served by only 20% of the nation's doctors. People travel 
over 100 kilometers just to reach a clinic, spending 70–80% of 
their household budget on the journey alone. Healthcare access 
is not a knowledge problem — it is a distance, cost, and 
awareness problem.

## Who Is Affected

- Rural communities across India, especially in states like 
  Uttarakhand and Himachal Pradesh
- Women who need financial permission even for basic medical visits
- Children under 5 dying from treatable diseases like malaria 
  and pneumonia simply because a clinic is too far
- Low income families who avoid hospitals because one visit 
  consumes most of their monthly income

## What I Am Building

A health symptom checker web platform that helps users in rural 
India identify their symptoms and find the right help. Users 
select their symptoms and receive guidance on Ayurvedic remedies 
for common conditions plus information about nearby government 
hospitals and health centres — using publicly available Indian 
government health data. The goal is zero cost, zero doctor 
required for first-level guidance, accessible on any phone.

## Current Status
